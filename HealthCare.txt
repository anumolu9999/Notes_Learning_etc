RVU's
In healthcare, Relative Value Units (RVUs) are not a single formula but a scaled value assigned to each medical service, calculated as the 
sum of three components: 
    Work RVUs, 
    Practice Expense (PE) RVUs, and 
    Malpractice (MP) RVUs. 
The total RVU for a service is then multiplied by a Conversion Factor to determine the payment for that service.

ICD-10 created by CMS and NCHS ((National Center for Health Statistics)
ICD-10 divided/consist of two medical code sets
1. Clinical Modification (ICD-10-CM) - Across all claims (Prof/inst IP/OP)
          Standardised classification system of diagnosis codes that represent conditions and disease, related health problems, abnoramal findings, signs and symptoms, injuries, external causes of injuries and diseases, and social circumstances
2. Procedure Coding System (ICD-10-PCS) (InPatient Procedures)
          Classification system of medical codes used in hospital settings to report inpatient procedures

Claims Pricing Context
	Scenario
Inpatient Hosp Claim - ICD10CM(Diagnosis)->ICD10 PCS (procedures inpatient) -> DRG -> Reimbursement
Outpatient/Prof claim - ICD10CM(diagnosis) -> CPT/HCPCS (op procedures) -> Fee Schedule

ICD-10-CM : S32.010 A (had period after 3rd char)
	3-7 characters - alphanumeric
	1st Char - A-Z except U (represents category - chapter or disease group)
	2nd/3rd char - 00-99 (represents category - disease or condition group)
	4th char - Number or alpha (represents Etiology)
	5th char - Number or alpha (represents Anatomic site)
	6th char - Number or alpha (represents Severity)
	7th char - Number or alpha (represents extension/Obstetrics, injuries and external cause of injury)
	(optional)			often represents Episode of care

ICD-10-PCS: 7 Digit code alphanumeric
	1st Char - represents section
	2nd char - Body System
	3rd char - Root operation
	4th char - Body Part
	5th char - Approach
	6th char - Device
	7th char - Qualifier

CPT & HCPCS (Outpatient procedures)
	CPT (Level 1 codes): Current Procedural Terminology
		Introduced by AMA in 1966
		
		Category 1: Standard Codes (5 Digits)
			Used for most commonly performed procedures		
			divided into below six sections
				Evaluation and management 99201-99499
				Anesthesia 	00100-01999, 99100-99140
				Surgery - 10021-69990
				Radiology - 70010-79999
				Pathology and Laboratory - 80047-89398
				Medicine - 90281-99607
		
		Category 2: Performance Measurement (4 digits and followed by F)
			Quality reporting initiatives (VBC)
			Optional tracking codes for performance measures
		
		Category 3: Emerging Technology (4digits and followed by T)
			Temporary alphanumeric codes for new and developing tech, procedures/services	
			Typically used for procedures not yet widely performed
	
	HCPCS (Level II Codes): Healthcare Common Procedure Coding system
		Introduced for the procedures/supplies/services not covered in CPT	
		Introduced by CMS in 1980
			A Codes - Transportation, supplies, etc
			B Codes - Enteral and parenteral therapy
			E Codes - Durable Medical Equipment (DME)
			J Codes - Drugs (injections, infusions)
			L Codes - Orthotics and prosthetics
	
	Modifiers: Two character codes (numeric or alphanumeric) appended to CPT or HCPCS
		Provide additional details
		clarify circumstances
		Avoid denial or incorrect payment
		Ensure accurate reimbursement
		
	CPT Modifiers: Two digit number maintained by AMA
		53 discontinued procedure
        26: Indicates a professional service component. 
        50: Used for a bilateral procedure. 
        52: Indicates a reduced service. 
        24: Used with an E&M (Evaluation and Management) code to show an unrelated service during a post-operative period
		
	HCPCS Modifiers: Two char number maintained by CMS
		RT Right side
		LT Left side
		FA, F1-F9 - Fingers (left thumb through little finger)
		TA, T1-T9 - Toes (left great toe through little toe)
		E1-E4 - Eyelid (upper left to Lower right)
	
	
	DRG Classification System
		MS DRG - 750 DRGs. CMS is the developer, used for medicare
		APR DRG - 1300 DRGs. 3M is the develope, used across all private/public insurers
